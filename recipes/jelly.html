Jelly

Description

Jelly is a semi-solid food substance made from fruit juice, sugar, and sometimes pectin or gelatin. It is typically used as a spread on bread or toast or as a filling in desserts. Jelly can also refer to a clear or translucent fruit spread made by boiling fruit juice with sugar and pectin. It is commonly used in desserts and confectionaries.

Ingredients

  • 1 package (85g) of fruit-flavored gelatin
  • 2 cups of boiling water
  • 1 cup of cold water

Steps

  1. Begin by bringing 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan or kettle.

  2. While the water is heating, pour the contents of the fruit-flavored gelatin packet into a heat-safe bowl.

  3. Slowly and carefully pour the boiling water over the gelatin powder in the bowl. Stir continuously until the gelatin powder is completely dissolved.

  4. Once the gelatin is dissolved, add 1 cup of cold water to the mixture. Stir well to combine.

  5. Decide on the desired shape and serving vessels for your jelly. You can use individual molds, a large jelly mold, or even small cups.

  6. Lightly grease the inside of your chosen molds or cups with a non-stick spray or a small amount of oil.

  7. Pour the prepared jelly mixture into the greased molds or cups, dividing it evenly between them.

  8. Place the molds or cups in the refrigerator for the jelly to set. The time required for setting can vary, but it usually takes around 2-4 hours. Check the package instructions for specific guidance on setting times.

  9. Once the jelly is fully set, remove it from the refrigerator. If you used individual molds, you can gently tap them on the counter or immerse in warm water for a few seconds to help release the jelly. For cups, you can use a butter knife or the edge of a spoon to loosen the jelly from the sides.

  10. Carefully invert the molds or cups onto a plate or serving platter to release the jelly.

  11. Serve and enjoy your homemade jelly! You can garnish it with whipped cream, fruits, or any other toppings you prefer.
